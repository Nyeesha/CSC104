How She Started


Billie Eilish is an American singer and songwriter who became famous at a young age. She was born in Los Angeles in 2001 and was homeschooled. She grew up in a creative environment, surrounded by music and arts. Billie and her brother, Finneas, started making music together, and in 2015, Billie uploaded her song “Ocean Eyes” to SoundCloud. The song went viral, leading to her signing a record deal and releasing more music. By 2019, her debut album When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go? became a huge success, making her one of the biggest names in pop music.

h2>Why is She Popular?

Her music is known for being unique and different, often described as "haunting" or "eccentric." Billie’s voice is soft and ethereal, and her songs often touch on deeper themes like mental health, addiction, and environmental issues, which are not typically seen in mainstream pop music. She has named artists like Lana Del Rey, Justin Bieber, and Amy Winehouse as influences, which shows how her style blends different genres and feels personal.

Her Motivation


Besides her music, Billie is also known for her activism. She speaks openly about her struggles with Tourette’s Syndrome and mental health and has worked to raise awareness on these issues. Billie is also passionate about fighting climate change and has supported youth movements like the ones led by Greta Thunberg. At her concerts, she even sets up "Eco-Villages" where fans can learn how to get involved in the climate fight.
